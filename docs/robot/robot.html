Death by Desire

Exploring Mechanics of Addiction

In order to explore the conceptual mechanics of addiction, I created a robot whose only goal or "desire" is to follow a being in front of it. However, if the robot is physically touched by a person, it dies and stops all functions. I wanted to translate the idea of endlessly pursuing or chasing something that you know brings you harm, such as addictive or abusive relationships.

Building

In order to achieve sensor-controlled movement I began by connecting DC motors to leg hinges and motor boxes provided by the Tamiya Stag Beetle robot starter kit. I then connected these motors to my Arduino microprocessor and coded them to move using the Arduino as the controller. After this I connective a capacitive sensor to the robot so that if touched by a human, the motors stop, it stops looking for a direction to move, its animated LED's turn off, and it prints "DEAD" to the console forever.

After, I connected two ultrasonic sensors to a breadboard facing in opposite direction. I programmed them to read in their respective distances to objects within a 30 degree radius of their vision, compare their distances from objects, and determine to move left or right depending on which sensor was closest to an object in its field. I then programmed this calculation to drive which motor was moved so the machine can in effect follow a human interacting with it.

Takeaway

By giving the robot limited goals and laborious movements, it appears pathetic and weak, even cute. I found myself establishing a loving yet patronizing disposition towards the robot. The walking, crawling motion of it's motors and hinge mechanisms led me to even anthropomorphize it's ultrasonic sensors, which now resemble four large eyes. Furthermore, I caught myself gendering my machine, referring to it as "she." By creating a subservient robot I reflected on my own human disposition to naturally feel dominant over seemingly "cute" machines. In addition, by programming the death of my own robot, I contemplated the human role in. perpetuating this dominance through manufacturing life cycles and death mechanics.

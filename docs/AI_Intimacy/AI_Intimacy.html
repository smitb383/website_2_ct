2D Study

Neural Networks + Natural Language Processing

To gain a deeper understand of the ways in which machines breakdown and attribute meaning to human language, I embarked on a quest to visualize neural networks and their relationship with natural language processing, and quickly realized it is neither concrete nor tangible in the way I was imagining.  I made three visualizations to compile my findings on word embedding, neural network structures, and natural language processing. After further research I organized these system drawings within the black-box paradigm of machine learning. Each exists "underneath" the black box, and as you go further from the shell, the processes go deeper into the structure of the machine learning and NLP structures. I printed each visualized system on transparent velum so that while hierarchical, they all appear and operate actively in tandem just as these systems do in the real world.

In attempting the visualize these absract concepts, I struggled in understanding the relationship between these different elements and processes and how they operated as a system. After an email dialogue with two data science specialists at Microsoft about how these parts interconnect, I was told, "they aren’t trivial questions and the answers aren’t trivial either" and that "the context is super important…and nuanced". In relinquishing my original plan to visualize these processes in a linear way, I gained a deeper understanding of the complexity of these systems and their integration as well as a visual organization of my learning research

3D Study

Aesthetics and Anatomy of the Ear

To continue to investigate AI and intimacy, I explored how machines collect sensory input in relationship to humans. Additionally I aimed to explore the relationship between aesthetics of sensory inputs and human responsiveness to humanoid objects. I 3D modeled and printed an ear and created a circuit that connects a sound sensor to an Arduino microprocessor in order to create an ear that "can hear". In studying the internal processes of sound to computation in humans and computers, I realized that the processes are based on the input - output black-box paradigm that governs not only machine learning but also understanding of the human brain. I therefore separated my external and internal ears with a partially open black box to show the separation in functional and aesthetic perception.

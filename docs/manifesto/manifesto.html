Design Manifesto

Actively Exploring Black-Box Systems

I wrote a manifesto outlining my core ethical and moral axioms of technological inclusion and ethically-aligned design, to guide my own design/technology practice. What started as a collection of my hopes and challenges for the techno-future, turned into a call to action; encouraging the active investigation of technological systems and an active contemplation of one’s own desired techno-future. I then physically manifested this writing into a black box-puzzle.

Written

*** scroll to read ***
We must not ask what WILL happen but what SHOULD happen?
We must be concerned with direction rather than magnitude of technological changes; changing ones direction when in motion rather than one's speed can take far less effort and can have far more powerful effects.
We must not qualify contemplations of should-happen with our projected and passive guesses of will-happen. We must  not be discouraged by our own or collective  inability to answer questions of should-happen but rather view these contemplations as a meditative practice; something that does not necessarily have a definitive end, answer, or solution but adds to a greater collective consciousness of these issues
We must not accept the input-output black box paradigms associated with  technologies of the present and future at face value.
We must redesign techno-systems for transparency; enabling the masses to understand their entanglement with information that is received, engaged, and left behind.
We must aim to break open black box systems and learn the inner workings of their mechanics, rather than sheepishly deem them too complex for our own understanding. We must seek out educational opportunities and knowledge to further these contemplations towards a robust and diverse understanding of the new frontier of technological change.
We must recognize our naiveté in understanding the timeline and implementation of possible futures  but understand there is no physical limit to manifesting them.
We must open our definitions of consciousness  to consider the future of life, but we must not be so foolish as to anthropomorphize machines within our experience and worldview
We must recognize technology's potential for intelligence, power, desire, agency, and control. We must release our convictions of present humanity as the finalized and dominant force of life, but rather ask what humanity values? What makes humanity special? What is worth preserving? And finally, investigate how might we get there
There is a vast chasm between fear of the unknown  and fear that disables, petrifies, and renders the beholder incapable or unwilling to respond to it.
We must not accept a fate that is dictated by a lack of action due to fear but a collaborative effort responding to that fear, uncertainty, and anticipation.
We must not be tricked by the mask of bliss that covers ignorance. Yearnings to push aside future technological considerations and live entirely in the here-and-now  will not be tolerated and seen as regressive and destructive

Physical

By creating a physical form that requires active translating, converting, and pattern recognition in a, literal, black box puzzle, I aimed to manifest the active investigation of technological black-box systems that I promote in my written manifesto.

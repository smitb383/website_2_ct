Chameleon Exhibition

Speculative Tech Curation & VR

Using maya and unity, I built and curated a speculative virtual, interactive exhibition as an attempt to analyze the events and technologies that could leed to a tumultuous rise of machine translation as a tool for manipulation. Using created and pre-made 3D assets, unity interactive installations, and the Oculus rift, this virtual exhibition follows the history and rise of Chameleon Technologies, a machine translation company of my imagination. Curating and building this exhibition allowed me to consider the trajectory of technological advancments in machine translation and its entanglements and relationship to other emerging technologies.

I completed a series of case studies prior to creating my exhibition to research, map, and speculate machine translation as it exists now and how this technology might evolve. I then made a speculative product, a machine translation software for AR called Chameleon, that allowed me to consider machine translation's near future applications and it's potential relationship with mainstream society. Through these making-based research studies, I was able to forecast the potential manifestations and impacts of machine translation in the near and distant future and have a more robust, researched understanding of the technology when curating my speculative exhibition.

Speculative MT

Hypothesizing the Future of Machine Translation

To investigate and consider the potential of machine translation as an emerging technology, I completed a series of case studies to research, map, and speculate machine translation as it exists now and how this technology might evolve. I began by making a system map considering the present and future entanglement of machine translation. I then made a speculative product, a machine translation software for AR called Chameleon, that allowed me situate and consider near future applications of what machine translation could look like and how this may effect and be incorporated into society. Lastly, I created a virtual museum exhibition that marked the evolution of this product and its trajectory into a destructive technology. Through these making-based research studies, I was able to forecast the potential manifestations and impacts of machines translat6ion in the near and distant future.

Chameleon Speculative Product

I created a speculative machine translation app that imagines the ability to pick up a Japanese book and seeing the text in English in real time, or walking around a foreign city and being able to read every sign, piece of information, and menu in your native language without taking out your phone, opening a translating app, plugging in the languages to translate between, taking a scan, and reading in the sub-par result. Chameleon offers you the ability to do this via AR image processing in combination with deep learning neural network based machine translation. Chameleon scans, detects, and coverts text to the users native language in real time without any necessary input from the user other than their eye position relative to their environment. Chameleon can transform text to appear in to the language you speak bringing to life everything from books to entire cityscapes that you previously would not be able to comprehend or read as a native speaker.

Speculative Disaster Museam

For my final study, I built a virtual museam exhibition in unity to speculate the destructuve potential of machine learning. The museam follows the history and rise of Chameleon Technologies and the technological advancements that enabled its birth. This virtual exhibition examines potential partnerships and societal climate that could enable Chameleon software to become a biologically and culturally integrated platform. Building this exhibition allowed me to consider the trajectory of technological advancments in machine transltion and its entanglements and relationship to other emerging technologies. This exhibition has been curated with the intention to analyze the events and technologies that could led to a tumultuous rise of machine translation as a tool for manipulation.
